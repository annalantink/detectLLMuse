Imagine you’re in a room, staring at obvious line matches, but everyone else picks the wrong one—turns out, they’re actors in Solomon Asch’s clever 1950s setup. About 37% of unsuspecting participants ditched their own clear vision to blend with the group’s bad call, proving social pressure’s sneaky power. These experiments lit up how we bend to fit in, shaping psych studies on everything from teen peer pressure to big group decisions.
